SIDC is a methodology — a structured process for designing, documenting, testing, and maintaining an investment strategy. It is not a fund, a product, or a specific set of securities. Think of it as a framework, like Agile for software development, applied to personal or institutional investing.
No. SIDC is designed to be accessible to individual investors at any experience level. Stage 1 (Plan) requires only basic financial literacy. The methodology becomes progressively more technical at Stages 3–4, but every step produces a concrete output — you always know what you've accomplished.
The first cycle typically takes 4–8 hours of focused work over 1–2 weeks. Stage 1 (Plan) is usually 1–2 hours. Stage 2 (Architecture) is 2–3 hours. Stages 3 and 4 together typically take 3–4 hours. Stage 5 (Monitoring) is ongoing — once set up, it requires 30–60 minutes per month for routine review.
Portfolio technical debt refers to the accumulated weight of undocumented decisions, emotional overrides, and untested assumptions in your investment approach. Just like software debt, it doesn't harm you immediately — but it compounds. When a market shock arrives, debt-laden portfolios fail structurally, not just because of bad luck.
